Personal Reflection

Making a website on my own was a really interesting and a challenging experience. I've been in a position when I've wanted to stop and give up because of stress. As the person I am, I never gave up and pushed through to the end, but not with a minimum of work, but with the greatest possible standards. Now, at this time and date of 03/10/21, I am close to finishing the website and have made my final presentation, which I will give on them on 15/10/21.

I am quite confident about submitting my work on October 13th, with a few more edits, because I have studied the website and pages and confirmed that I utilised the criteria to evaluate my work. I also modified the structure of the website, as well as the pictures and colours.


Working alone

At first look, working on a website by yourself while it is intended to have three people sounds like a nightmare and puts you at a disadvantage. That's what I thought at first when I realised I'd be working with myself. Now that I've worked alone, I'm pleased I did because it taught me that if I set my mind to it, I can do almost anything. I developed this website that I am really pleased with after many late nights and endless hours of work. When I say all of this, I don't want to come out as petty, but I'm glad I was able to work with myself because it has broadened my horizons in terms of what I can do.


Challenges

Lack of members, workload, and research were among the obstacles I faced. As I indicated in "Working Alone," there were numerous times when I needed assistance but could only rely on myself to accomplish so. The workload, aside from the lack of members, was the implementation of the website; I had to spend several hours ensuring the website met the highest standards, which included modifying and relocating text and graphics to make it more intelligible. As a result, I found myself doing more work, so I'll have to be more disciplined and organised in the future. It was also tough to conduct research because many websites mention Neuroalink.


Website

I wanted to make a website that was simple but interesting to look at as I was building it. As a result, I've chosen vivid and inviting colours to convey warmth and comfort on the website, encouraging visitors to read. I then proceeded to build text boxes, which will make reading much simpler while also improving the website's appearance.


Ethics
